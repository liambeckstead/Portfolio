Who I Am

About Me

I'm a pre-business student at Brigham Young University applying to the BYU Marriott School of Business, pursuing a Business Management degree alongside minors in Healthcare Leadership and American Indian Studies. I grew up in New Mexico, and my long-term goal is to return there as an aspiring physician — specifically to serve Navajo communities in the region.

My path to medicine is intentionally non-traditional. Understanding how hospitals operate — budgeting, staffing, administration — will make me a more dynamic and effective advocate for my patients. My American Indian Studies minor gives me the historical and cultural context to provide care that is genuinely informed by the lived experience of the people I hope to serve.

I've completed a year of Navajo language study and am conversational. Language is trust, and trust is the foundation of good medicine.

Why Business + Medicine?

Most physicians enter practice without ever studying how a hospital actually works. By earning a business degree alongside my pre-med requirements, I aim to understand budget cycles, staffing decisions, and operational constraints — so that as an aspiring physician, I can advocate more effectively for patients and staff alike. A physician who understands the business of medicine can change more than just their patients; they can change the systems those patients depend on.
